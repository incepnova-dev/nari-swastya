Empower Your Wellness

Learn evidence-based techniques to manage symptoms, boost your mental health, and take control of your wellbeing when symptoms strike

Mindfulness & Meditation

Practice mindful breathing and meditation to reduce stress, manage pain, and improve emotional wellbeing.

1

Find a quiet space and sit comfortably

2

Close your eyes, breathe deeply for 5 counts

3

Focus on your breath, letting thoughts pass

4

Practice 10-15 minutes daily for best results

Benefits:

  • Reduces anxiety and depression
  • Lowers cortisol (stress hormone)
  • Improves pain tolerance
  • Enhances sleep quality

Gentle Movement & Exercise

Low-impact exercises tailored for symptom management that boost endorphins and improve overall health.

1

Start with 10-minute gentle walks daily

2

Try yoga or stretching for flexibility

3

Build up gradually - listen to your body

4

Aim for 150 minutes moderate activity/week

Benefits:

  • Releases natural pain-relieving endorphins
  • Improves insulin sensitivity (PCOS, diabetes)
  • Strengthens cardiovascular health
  • Boosts mood and energy levels

Anti-Inflammatory Nutrition

Evidence-based dietary approaches to reduce inflammation, balance hormones, and manage symptoms.

1

Focus on whole foods, vegetables, fruits

2

Include omega-3 fatty acids (fish, nuts)

3

Limit processed foods, sugar, trans fats

4

Stay hydrated - 8-10 glasses water daily

Benefits:

  • Reduces chronic inflammation
  • Balances blood sugar (PCOS, diabetes)
  • Supports healthy weight management
  • Improves gut health and immunity

Sleep Optimization

Master sleep hygiene techniques to improve rest quality, hormone regulation, and overall recovery.

1

Maintain consistent sleep schedule (7-9 hrs)

2

Create dark, cool bedroom environment

3

Avoid screens 1 hour before bed

4

Try relaxation techniques before sleep

Benefits:

  • Regulates hormones (cortisol, insulin)
  • Enhances immune function
  • Improves mood and cognitive function
  • Supports tissue repair and healing

Stress Management

Learn proven techniques to manage stress, anxiety, and emotional overwhelm during difficult times.

1

Practice daily journaling (5-10 minutes)

2

Use progressive muscle relaxation

3

Set boundaries and learn to say "no"

4

Connect with support network regularly

Benefits:

  • Lowers blood pressure and heart rate
  • Reduces inflammation markers
  • Improves emotional resilience
  • Enhances overall quality of life

Natural Pain Management

Non-pharmacological approaches to manage chronic pain and discomfort from various conditions.

1

Apply heat therapy for cramps/muscle pain

2

Try cold therapy for inflammation

3

Use TENS units or acupressure

4

Practice gentle stretching and yoga

Benefits:

  • Reduces reliance on pain medications
  • Improves circulation and healing
  • Decreases muscle tension
  • Enhances overall comfort and mobility

Evidence-Based Preventive Screening

Recommended screening schedules from USPSTF, ACOG, ICMR, and WHO — know exactly when and what to screen for at every life stage

Cervical Cancer

ACOG • WHO • IARC
  • Age 21–29 Pap smear every 3 years
  • Age 30–65 Pap + HPV co-test every 5 years or Pap alone every 3 years
  • Age 65+ Discontinue if adequate prior negative screening
  • HIV+ Annual Pap regardless of age
HPV vaccination (9–45 yrs) reduces risk by up to 90% — ACOG Practice Bulletin #168 (2022)

Breast Cancer

USPSTF 2024 • ACOG • NCCN
  • Age 40–74 Mammogram every 2 years (USPSTF 2024 Grade B)
  • High-risk Annual MRI + mammogram from age 30 (BRCA1/2, family history)
  • All ages Monthly breast self-exam; annual clinical breast exam
  • ICMR India Clinical breast exam every 2–3 years from age 30
BRCA genetic counseling if first-degree relative affected — NCCN Guidelines v2.2024

Cardiovascular Risk

ACC/AHA • WHO • USPSTF
  • Age ≥20 Blood pressure check every 1–2 years
  • Age ≥20 Fasting lipid panel every 4–6 years; more often if risk factors present
  • Age 40–75 10-year CVD risk calculation (Pooled Cohort Equation)
  • Post-menopause Annual lipid and glucose monitoring — AHA 2023
CVD is the leading cause of death in women globally — WHO Global Health Estimates 2024

Diabetes & Metabolic

ADA 2024 • ICMR • USPSTF
  • Age ≥35 Fasting blood glucose or HbA1c every 3 years
  • PCOS Oral glucose tolerance test every 3–5 years — ESHRE 2023
  • Gestational DM 75g OGTT at 6–12 weeks postpartum; then every 3 years
  • ICMR India Screen from age 30 if BMI ≥23 or family history
India has 101 million diabetics — ICMR-INDIAB Study 2023 (Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology)

Bone Density (Osteoporosis)

NOF • USPSTF • ACOG
  • Age ≥65 DEXA bone density scan — USPSTF Grade B
  • <65 yrs DEXA if postmenopausal with fracture risk factors (FRAX score)
  • Premature menopause DEXA immediately at diagnosis
  • All women Calcium 1200mg/day + Vitamin D 600–800 IU/day — NOF 2024
1 in 3 women over 50 will suffer an osteoporotic fracture — IOF World Osteoporosis Report 2023

Mental Health

WHO • USPSTF • ACOG
  • All adults Depression screening with PHQ-9 at every annual visit — USPSTF 2023
  • Perinatal EPDS (Edinburgh scale) at 1st prenatal visit, 28–36 weeks, 4–8 weeks postpartum — ACOG 2023
  • Adolescents Anxiety screening with GAD-7 annually from age 13 — USPSTF 2023
  • Menopause Screen for depression and cognitive changes — IMS 2022
Women are 2× more likely than men to experience depression — WHO Mental Health Atlas 2022

All screening intervals are population-level recommendations. Individual intervals may vary based on personal and family medical history. Always confirm your screening schedule with your healthcare provider. Sources: USPSTF 2024, ACOG Practice Bulletins 2022–2024, ICMR Guidelines, WHO 2023, ADA Standards of Medical Care 2024.

Red Flag Symptoms

Evidence-based triage guidance from ACOG, WHO, and emergency medicine guidelines — know when your symptoms require immediate action versus scheduled care

Call Emergency (112/911) Immediately

Life-Threatening
  • Chest pain, arm/jaw pain, or sudden breathlessness (cardiac event)
  • Sudden severe headache "worst of my life" (subarachnoid haemorrhage)
  • Heavy vaginal bleeding soaking >1 pad/hour for 2 consecutive hours
  • Severe abdominal pain with rigidity (ectopic rupture, peritonitis)
  • Sudden one-sided weakness, facial droop, slurred speech (stroke — FAST)
  • Loss of consciousness, seizure, or altered mental status
ACOG Practice Bulletin #183 | AHA Stroke Guidelines 2023 | WHO Emergency Triage

See a Doctor Within 24–48 Hours

Urgent
  • Fever >38.5°C with pelvic pain or foul-smelling discharge (PID/endometritis)
  • Sudden breast lump, skin dimpling, or nipple inversion
  • Post-menopausal vaginal bleeding (any amount)
  • Severe headache + blurred vision + swelling in pregnancy (pre-eclampsia)
  • Painful, swollen leg with redness (deep vein thrombosis)
  • Unexplained weight loss >5% body weight in <6 months
  • Severe mood changes or suicidal thoughts (perinatal or otherwise)
NICE Guidelines NG116 | ACOG Hypertension in Pregnancy 2022 | WHO Integrated Care

Schedule Appointment (1–2 Weeks)

Non-Urgent but Important
  • Irregular periods (absent >3 cycles, cycles <21 or >35 days)
  • Persistent pelvic pain lasting >6 months (possible endometriosis)
  • Significant facial hair, acne, or scalp hair loss (PCOS screening)
  • Painful intercourse (dyspareunia) — may indicate endometriosis or vaginismus
  • Urinary incontinence or prolapse symptoms
  • Persistent bloating, early satiety, or pelvic pressure (ovarian cancer risk)
RCOG Green-top Guideline | ACOG PCOS Guidelines 2023 | ESHRE Endometriosis 2022
