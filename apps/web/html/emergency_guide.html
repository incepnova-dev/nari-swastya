Life-Threatening Emergencies

Call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room immediately if you experience any of these symptoms.

ü´Ä

Preeclampsia Crisis

Red Flag Symptoms:

  • Severe headache that won't go away
  • Visual disturbances (flashing lights, spots)
  • Upper right abdominal pain
  • Sudden swelling of face and hands
  • Sudden weight gain (>5 lbs in a week)
Action: Call 911 immediately. This can lead to seizures (eclampsia) and is life-threatening.
ü©∏

Severe Hemorrhage

Red Flag Symptoms:

  • Soaking through 1+ pad per hour
  • Passing large clots (larger than golf ball)
  • Dizziness or fainting
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Cold, clammy skin
Action: Go to ER now. Severe bleeding can lead to shock and requires immediate intervention.
üíî

Miscarriage Emergency

Red Flag Symptoms:

  • Heavy bleeding with severe cramping
  • Tissue passing with blood
  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Fever with bleeding
  • Foul-smelling discharge
Action: Seek immediate care. Risk of infection or incomplete miscarriage requiring intervention.
ü´Å

Severe Respiratory Distress

Red Flag Symptoms:

  • Difficulty breathing or catching breath
  • Chest pain with breathing
  • Blue lips or fingertips
  • Rapid breathing (>30 breaths/min)
  • Wheezing or gasping
Action: Call 911. Could indicate pulmonary embolism, severe asthma, or heart issues.
üß†

Mental Health Crisis

Red Flag Symptoms:

  • Thoughts of harming self or baby
  • Hallucinations or delusions
  • Severe confusion or disorientation
  • Extreme paranoia
  • Unable to care for self or baby
Action: Call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or go to ER. Postpartum psychosis is a medical emergency.

Severe Infection

Red Flag Symptoms:

  • Fever >103¬∞F (39.4¬∞C)
  • Chills and shaking
  • Rapid heartbeat (>120 bpm)
  • Severe pain (mastitis, incision)
  • Red streaking from wound or breast
Action: Go to ER. Sepsis can develop rapidly and is life-threatening without treatment.

Urgent But Not Emergency

These symptoms require medical attention soon, but you have time to contact your provider during business hours or use an urgent care clinic.

  • Fever 100.4-103¬∞F (38-39.4¬∞C)

Mastitis Symptoms

  • Breast pain with redness
  • Fever and flu-like symptoms
  • Warm, swollen area on breast
Call within 24h. Antibiotics usually needed to prevent abscess.

Blood Sugar Issues (GDM)

  • Consistently high readings (>200 mg/dL)
  • Very low readings (<60 mg/dL)
  • Symptoms not improving with treatment
Call your OB or endocrinologist within 24h for adjustment.

Severe Depression/Anxiety

  • Unable to function or care for baby
  • Panic attacks or extreme anxiety
  • Overwhelming sadness for 2+ weeks
Call your OB or mental health provider. This needs treatment.

Self-Care and Watch

These symptoms can often be managed at home with monitoring. Call if they worsen or persist beyond 48 hours.

ü§¢

Mild Nausea/Vomiting

When to monitor:
  • Can keep some fluids down
  • No severe dehydration
  • Occasional vomiting only
Self-care:
  • Small, frequent meals
  • Ginger tea or candies
  • Stay hydrated
  • Rest when needed
‚ö†Ô∏è Call if: Can't keep fluids down for 12+ hours
üò¥

Fatigue & Mild Mood Changes

When to monitor:
  • Feeling tired but functioning
  • Mild baby blues (first 2 weeks)
  • Still bonding with baby
Self-care:
  • Accept help from others
  • Nap when baby naps
  • Talk to supportive friends
  • Light exercise/fresh air
‚ö†Ô∏è Call if: Not improving after 2 weeks or worsening
ü©π

Minor Incision Discomfort

When to monitor:
  • Mild redness around edges
  • Normal healing pain
  • No drainage or foul smell
Self-care:
  • Keep incision clean and dry
  • Wear loose clothing
  • Take prescribed pain meds
  • Avoid heavy lifting
‚ö†Ô∏è Call if: Increasing redness, pus, or opening of incision
ü§±

Mild Breastfeeding Issues

When to monitor:
  • Sore nipples without cracks
  • Mild engorgement
  • Baby learning to latch
Self-care:
  • Ensure proper latch
  • Use lanolin cream
  • Apply warm compresses
  • Feed on demand
‚ö†Ô∏è Call if: Fever, severe pain, or red streaking on breast
üåä

Mild Swelling

When to monitor:
  • Gradual, mild swelling
  • Improves with elevation
  • Both sides equal
Self-care:
  • Elevate legs when sitting
  • Avoid standing long periods
  • Stay hydrated
  • Wear compression socks
‚ö†Ô∏è Call if: Sudden swelling of face/hands or one-sided leg swelling
ü§ï

Mild Headaches

When to monitor:
  • Tension-type headaches
  • Responds to rest/hydration
  • No visual changes
Self-care:
  • Stay hydrated
  • Rest in dark, quiet room
  • Cold compress on forehead
  • Acetaminophen if approved
‚ö†Ô∏è Call if: Severe, sudden, or with vision changes
