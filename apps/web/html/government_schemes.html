Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY)

Pregnancy

Cash assistance for institutional delivery. Promotes safe childbirth and reduces maternal mortality.

  • ₹1,400 cash for urban delivery
  • ₹1,600 cash for rural delivery
  • Free institutional delivery
  • Transport allowance provided

Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY)

Pregnancy

Direct cash transfer for pregnant and lactating mothers for improved nutrition and health.

  • ₹5,000 in three installments
  • For first living child only
  • Nutrition support during pregnancy
  • Direct bank transfer

Ayushman Bharat - PM-JAY

Family

World's largest health insurance scheme providing coverage up to ₹5 lakh per family per year.

  • ₹5 lakh coverage per family/year
  • 1,400+ procedures covered
  • Cashless hospitalization
  • Pre and post-hospitalization

National Health Mission (NHM)

Preventive

Comprehensive health services including maternal, child health, and disease control programs.

  • Free antenatal care services
  • Immunization programs
  • Family planning services
  • Disease prevention programs

Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK)

Kids

Free delivery and care for pregnant women and sick newborns up to one year.

  • Free delivery in public facilities
  • Free cesarean section
  • Free medicines and diagnostics
  • Sick newborn care up to 1 year

Mission Indradhanush

Kids

Immunization program ensuring full vaccination coverage for all children and pregnant women.

  • 12 vaccine-preventable diseases
  • Free immunization services
  • Regular vaccination drives
  • Track vaccination schedule

POSHAN Abhiyaan

Preventive

Flagship program to reduce malnutrition, anemia, low birth weight and stunting among pregnant women, lactating mothers and children under 6 years.

  • Growth monitoring for children under 6 years
  • Nutrition counseling for pregnant and lactating women
  • Supplementary nutrition through Anganwadi centres
  • Community-based events on diet and breastfeeding

Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS)

Kids

Early childhood care, nutrition and pre-school education for children under 6 and health services for pregnant and lactating women.

  • Supplementary nutrition for children 0–6 years
  • Pre-school education for 3–6 years
  • Health check-ups and referral services
  • Nutrition and health education for mothers

LaQshya – Labour Room Quality Improvement

Pregnancy

Quality improvement initiative to ensure respectful, safe and complication-free care during childbirth in public facilities.

  • Upgraded labour rooms and maternity OTs
  • Standard protocols for normal and high-risk delivery
  • Timely referral for obstetric emergencies
  • Respectful maternity care standards

Maternal Health – Emergency Obstetric Care (EmOC)

Preventive

Network of First Referral Units and higher centres to handle high-risk pregnancies and emergency obstetric surgeries.

  • 24×7 Caesarean and obstetric surgery services
  • Blood transfusion and ICU support where available
  • Skilled obstetrician and anesthetist teams
  • Referral transport for emergency cases

Maternal and Newborn Health (RMNCAH+N)

Preventive

Package of antenatal, intrapartum and postnatal services to reduce maternal and newborn deaths and complications.

  • Recommended ANC check-ups and lab tests
  • Skilled birth attendance and safe delivery
  • Essential newborn care and breastfeeding support
  • Postpartum home visits by frontline workers

Anemia Mukt Bharat (AMB)

Preventive

Intensified strategy to reduce anemia in women of reproductive age, pregnant women, adolescents and young children.

  • Iron-folic acid supplementation for pregnant women
  • Deworming for women and children
  • Point-of-care hemoglobin testing at facilities
  • Counseling on iron-rich diet and spacing of births

SNCU / NBSU Newborn Care

Kids

Special Newborn Care Units and Newborn Stabilization Units for sick and low-birth-weight babies in public hospitals.

  • Free intensive care for sick newborns
  • Support for preterm and low-birth-weight babies
  • Specialized neonatal equipment and trained staff
  • Linkage with follow-up and early intervention services

Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)

Kids

Child health screening and early intervention to detect birth defects, diseases and developmental delays from 0–18 years.

  • Screening at schools, Anganwadis and facilities
  • Free treatment for selected conditions
  • Referral to District Early Intervention Centres
  • Focus on developmental and learning issues

Rashtriya Kishor Swasthya Karyakram (RKSK)

Preventive

Adolescent health program focusing on nutrition, mental health, menstrual hygiene and reproductive health for girls and boys 10–19 years.

  • Adolescent-friendly health clinics
  • Weekly iron-folic acid supplementation in schools
  • Counseling on menstrual hygiene and safe relationships
  • Peer educator-led community sessions

Janani Express / 108 Ambulance Services

Pregnancy

Free ambulance and referral transport for pregnant women, newborns and emergency maternal cases to public health facilities.

  • 24×7 toll-free emergency number
  • Home-to-facility and inter-facility transfers
  • Basic life support in transit
  • Priority for pregnant women and newborns

Ayushman Bharat – Health and Wellness Centres (AB-HWC)

Family

Upgraded Sub-Centres and PHCs providing free comprehensive primary care for women, children and families close to home.

  • Antenatal, postnatal and newborn care
  • Screening for NCDs, anemia and mental health
  • Family planning and counseling services
  • Linkage to higher centres for surgery and admissions

Mamata / MDM-like Hot Cooked Meal for Pregnant Women

Pregnancy

State-linked scheme model that provides hot cooked meals or take-home rations to pregnant and lactating women through Anganwadi centres.

  • One additional meal during pregnancy and lactation
  • Ensures minimum calorie and protein intake
  • Often combined with health and nutrition education
  • Reduces risk of low birth weight and anemia

Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP)

Kids

National campaign to improve survival, protection and education of the girl child and correct declining child sex ratio.

  • Awareness on preventing sex-selective practices
  • Support for girl child education initiatives
  • Convergence with scholarships and savings schemes
  • Community mobilization for protecting girls

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

Kids

Long-term savings scheme for parents of a girl child to support future education and marriage expenses with tax benefits.

  • Account can be opened for girls below 10 years
  • Attractive interest rate notified by Government
  • Income tax benefits on deposits
  • Partial withdrawal for higher education

Mid-Day Meal / PM POSHAN for School Children

Family

Nutritious cooked meals in schools to improve child nutrition, learning outcomes and reduce gender gaps in enrolment.

  • Free hot cooked lunch on school days
  • Calorie and protein norms by age group
  • Special focus on girls and disadvantaged groups
  • Platform for deworming and health education

Mother and Child Tracking System / RCH Portal

Pregnancy

Digital tracking of pregnant women and children to ensure timely antenatal, delivery and immunization services.

  • Registration of every pregnancy and newborn
  • SMS reminders for due services
  • Better follow-up for high-risk pregnancies
  • Improved coverage of vaccines and check-ups

Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS) – Women & Children

Family

Comprehensive health care including maternity, gynecological and pediatric services for eligible central government employees and dependents.

  • OPD and IPD care in empanelled hospitals
  • Coverage for obstetric and gynecological surgeries
  • Childhood immunization and specialist pediatric care
  • Cashless treatment as per CGHS package rates

National Policy for Rare Diseases – Financial Support

Family

Financial assistance for eligible children and adults with specified rare diseases requiring high-cost treatment or surgery.

  • Support up to a defined ceiling for one-time treatments
  • Treatment at designated Centres of Excellence
  • Benefit available in addition to other schemes
  • Special focus on children from low-income families

Ladli / Girl Child Protection Schemes (State Models)

Kids

State-backed conditional cash transfer and savings schemes that encourage birth, survival and education of the girl child.

  • Lump-sum benefits at key life stages (birth, schooling)
  • Incentives linked to immunization and school attendance
  • Support for education till secondary or higher
  • Higher benefits for girls from vulnerable groups

Safe Abortion and Family Planning Services (under NHM)

Preventive

Free or subsidized access to safe abortion, contraception and counseling at public facilities as per MTP Act provisions.

  • Safe procedures by trained providers
  • Range of contraceptive choices for women and couples
  • Post-abortion care and counseling
  • Protection of privacy and confidentiality

Schemes for Children with Disabilities (SAD / DDRC Models)

Kids

Financial and rehabilitation support for children with disabilities, including assistive devices, therapy and inclusive education support.

  • Free or subsidized assistive devices
  • Physiotherapy, speech therapy and counseling
  • Educational support and linkage to scholarships
  • Guidance for parents on home-based care

National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, CVD & Stroke (NPCDCS)

Preventive

Screening and early diagnosis of common cancers and NCDs among adults, with focus on women for breast, cervical and oral cancers.

  • Screening camps at HWCs and CHCs
  • Referral for diagnostic tests and biopsies
  • Linkage with schemes covering cancer surgery and treatment
  • Lifestyle counseling and follow-up care

State-level Maternity Assistance Schemes (e.g., Dr. Muthulakshmi / KCR / Mamta Models)

Pregnancy

Additional state maternity benefit schemes that top up central benefits for pregnant women opting for institutional delivery and child immunization.

  • Cash incentives linked to ANC visits and delivery
  • Installments tied to child immunization milestones
  • Extra support for SC/ST and BPL women
  • Often include nutrition kits or baby care kits

PM CARES for Children

Kids

Support for children who lost both parents or primary caregivers in specified emergencies, covering education, health insurance and livelihood support.

  • Free education and scholarship support
  • Health insurance cover up to a defined limit
  • Monthly stipend after 18 years
  • Lump-sum amount at 23 years for higher education or self-employment

Application Guide

Simple Steps to Access Benefits

Follow these easy steps to apply for government health schemes and start receiving benefits

1

Verify Eligibility

Check if you meet the income and residency requirements for the program

2

Gather Documents

Collect Aadhaar card, income proof, residence proof, and pregnancy documents

3

Visit Health Center

Go to your nearest government hospital or health center with documents

4

Submit Application

Fill out forms and submit with required documents to the designated officer

5

Receive Confirmation

Get acknowledgment receipt and wait for verification and approval

6

Access Benefits

Once approved, benefits will be transferred directly to your bank account
