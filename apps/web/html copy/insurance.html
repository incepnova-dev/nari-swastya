Coverage Basics

Build Your Mental Model

A clear understanding before you book any appointment—know your plan inside out.

🆔

Plan Type

HMO/PPO/EPO affects provider network and whether you need referrals for specialists.

What matters: Know if you can choose any doctor or need approval first.
🌐

In-Network vs Out-of-Network

In-network providers typically cost less and have simpler claims processing.

Save money: Always check provider network status before booking.
💰

Deductible & Copay

Know what you pay before coverage kicks in and your cost per visit.

Track it: Keep tabs on your deductible progress throughout the year.
📝

Prior Authorization

Some labs, imaging, or specialists may require pre-approval from your insurer.

Pro tip: Ask about this when scheduling—saves time and hassle.
Pre-Booking Checklist

Verify First, Book Second

Use this checklist before teleconsultation or finding specialists to avoid surprises.

✓

What to Verify

✅
Clinic/doctor is in-network

Confirm with both the provider and your insurance

✅
Referral needed?

Common in HMOs—get it from your PCP first

✅
Teleconsultation coverage

Video visits may have different copay rates

✅
Lab/ultrasound coverage

Check which facilities are in-network for testing

✅
Maternity coverage details

Prenatal visits, delivery, and postpartum care

✅
Preventive screening coverage

Pap smear, mammogram often covered at 100%

📞

Questions to Ask

1
Are you in-network for my plan and tier?

Essential first question—saves you money

2
What is my estimated patient responsibility?

Get a cost estimate before your visit

3
Do you bill as "office visit" or "telehealth"?

Different codes = different copays

4
Are labs/imaging billed separately?

Know all costs upfront, not just the visit

5
Do you need prior authorization or referral?

Avoid claim denials by asking early

Plan Comparison

Know Your Plan Type

Quick comparison of HMO, PPO, and EPO to understand what works for you.

HMO

Health Maintenance Organization

✓ Pros

  • Lower monthly premiums
  • Lower out-of-pocket costs
  • Coordinated care through PCP

✗ Cons

  • Referrals required for specialists
  • Limited to network providers
  • Less flexibility in choices
PPO

Preferred Provider Organization

✓ Pros

  • No referrals needed
  • Can see out-of-network providers
  • Maximum flexibility

✗ Cons

  • Higher monthly premiums
  • Higher deductibles
  • More paperwork for out-of-network
EPO

Exclusive Provider Organization

✓ Pros

  • No referrals for specialists
  • Lower premiums than PPO
  • Good network coverage

✗ Cons

  • No out-of-network coverage
  • Must stay in network
  • Limited to plan providers
